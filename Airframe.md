Custom CAD
----------
-This project uses a 3D printed airframe based on the Apex glider platform. 

-While the base STL files are provided by the aircraft designer, the project still requires extensive CAD-based mechanical integration and 3D printing work.

How CAD is used in this project
-------------------------------
-The aircraft is fully manufactured using FDM 3D printing, which requires working with CAD models and slicing software to prepare parts for printing. 

-This includes selecting appropriate print settings such as orientation, wall thickness, infill, and structural reinforcement to ensure the airframe is lightweight yet strong enough for flight.

Mechanical Integration Work
---------------------------

Significant CAD-related work is required to integrate all onboard electronics into the airframe. The aircraft must accommodate:

Flight controller and GPS module placement

Battery mounting and weight balancing

Solar panel placement on wings/fuselage

Telemetry radio positioning

ESP32-CAM placement for onboard vision

Wiring paths and component access

These integrations may require modifications, mounting solutions, and structural planning to ensure proper fit, weight distribution, cooling, and safe operation.

Purpose
-------

-The use of CAD and 3D printing allows the aircraft to be lightweight, customizable, and easily reproducible. 

-This makes it possible to build an autonomous solar-electric glider platform while maintaining flexibility for upgrades and experimentation.
